Weapon Bonuses

Arming Sword

Whenever you successfully parry with your arming sword, you may make a free attack with the arming sword. This free attack resets your cooldown.

Bardiche

This weapon can be braced for a charge. It takes 3 seconds to do so and deals double damage to charging foes if you hit.

Bellybow

All this weapon’s damage is counted as if it were on 1 dice for /D armor.

Recovery Advanced Trainings for this weapon remove 1.5 seconds (rounded down) off their Recovery per bonus instead of 1.

Bill

Damage with this weapon is effectively doubled for Knock-Backs vs mounted defenders.

Bludgeon

Damage with this weapon is effectively doubled for Trauma checks.

Composite Bow

This is the only mechanical ranged weapon that may be used from horseback.

This weapon requires two hands regardless of size.

Court Sword

When rolling damage, reroll the lowest die. It’s doesn’t explode.

Crossbow

If you steady this weapon against the ground or a stand, you may double your Attack bonus from training with this weapon.

Recovery Advanced Trainings for this weapon remove 1.5 seconds (rounded down) off their Recovery per bonus instead of 1.

Dagger

This weapon can be used as either a Slashing or Piercing weapon. You only need one Weapon Basic Training, and any Weapon Advanced Trainings count for both.

Estoc

When wielding this weapon two handed, damage from it is effectively dealt on 1 die.

Fire Lance

This weapon’s loud noise causes everyone to duck. Everyone within its range flinches for a number of seconds equal to 1 of the d10s, determined beforehand (see Terrain and Situational Factors section of Chapter 9). This includes the gunner as well.

This penalty is lowered by 1 for every combat a person has been in that involves firearms or is completely negated if they have Basic Weapon Training (Any Firearm).

If several firearms have gone off on the same second, you take the highest delay rolled. Additionally, those already stunned by a firearm when another goes off are unaffected by that firearm's delay.

This is a single use weapon.

Guisarme

Damage with this weapon is effectively doubled for Knock-Backs vs mounted defenders.

Halberd

This weapon can be used as either a Slashing or Piercing weapon. You only need one Weapon Basic Training, and any Weapon Advanced Trainings count for both.

Hakenbuchse

If you steady this weapon against the ground or a stand, you may double your Attack bonus from training with this weapon.

Recovery Advanced Trainings for this weapon remove 1.5 seconds (rounded down) off their Recovery per bonus instead of 1.

This weapon also comes with a drop-down pole that takes 5 seconds to deploy or stow away.

This weapon’s loud noise causes everyone to duck. Everyone within the first two range categories of this weapon flinches for a number of seconds equal to what the smaller of its two damage dice rolls (see Terrain and Situational Factors section of Chapter 9). If they’re still surprised, they can act after those seconds are over. This includes the gunner as well.

This penalty is lowered by 1 for every combat a person has been in that involves firearms or is completely negated if they have Basic Weapon Training (Any Firearm).

If several firearms have gone off on the same second, you take the highest delay rolled. Additionally, those already stunned by a firearm when another goes off are unaffected by that firearm's delay.

This weapon Backfires (see sidebar).

Back Firing

On a roll of 1 on your attack dice, this weapon jams, making it unusable until repaired.

On top of this, you need to roll another d20. On the roll of 20, the gun still goes off but does half damage and the firing mechanism is broken so the firearm is unusable until repaired.

On the roll of a second 1, the gun backfires, dealing half damage to you. At least, it’s not jammed?

Horsemans Pick

This weapon is designed for use on horseback. Double its damage dice while mounted.

Javelin

If this weapon strikes a shield. and its damage is greater than the shield’s DR, the javelin has gotten lodged in the shield’s surface.

The Defender must either spend 2d6! seconds pulling it out or defend with a base 1d12! for as long as he continues to use the shield.

Unlike other ranged weapons, you add your Damage Modifier from Strength to your damage.

Unarmed

This is the character’s bare knuckles and so they pose less of a threat to those attacking with a weapon, meaning attackers double their weapon’s Recovery against unarmed opponents.

On explosions, the additional dice are at a -2, instead of the standard -1.

Warbow

This weapon must be used from a prone position, bracing the body of the bow with your legs. It takes 10 second - your armor's skill adjustment (so heaver armor makes it so you take longer to get up) to get up, but only 3 seconds to drop prone.
