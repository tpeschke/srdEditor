These rules add another layer of strategy to combat and should be implemented as soon as you have the basics down.

Each Tactic, at the top, when you can employ them and which other Tactics they can be combined with which. For “On Defense” and “On Attack”, the Tactic has to be declared before you roll.

Once committed to, Tactics can only be switched out on your next Attack or when you are no longer Engaged. So, if you’re Active Dodging, you have to commit to that Tactic on your defense but cannot stop Active Dodging until your next attack.

Active Dodging

On Defense

Compatible Attacks Hold Back

Compatible Defenses All except Brace Yourself, and Step Back

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

This move relies on actively focusing on dodging against incoming attacks.

A defender commits to how much he wants to focus on dodging incoming attacks, both melee and ranged, choosing from a bonus to his defense of either +3 or +5, but netting an equal penalty to his attacks (-3 or -5).

Additionally, this increases the range for Near Perfect Defenses by 1, so you score them on a base natural 18 - 19.

But focusing on defense does slow you down, and you are limited to 5 ft / second of movement.

Brace Yourself

On Defense

Compatible Attacks All except Charge, Feint, and In Their Guard

Compatible Defenses Choking Up, Fend Off, Long Guard

Maybe you look at your opponent and say "No, you move". You root your stance and ready yourself, sacrificing some maneuverability to hold the line.

While using this Tactic, a character adds +15 to their Knock-Back Threshold and gains a +5 to their Trauma Checks, however, they defend on a dice size 1 size smaller.

Called Shot

On Attack

Compatible Attacks Feint, In Their Guard

Compatible Defenses Brace Yourself, Choking Up, Fend Off, Hit the Deck

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

By carefully aiming your shot, a character can reduce the effects of heavy armor by taking more time to target gaps.

When employing this tactic, on a successful attack, a character halves their effective number of damage dice (rounded up) for considering /D DR, however, it takes a bit longer to set up the strike and so they increase their Recovery by 5 seconds.

Crushing weapons are unable to employ this tactic.

Careful Aiming

On Attack

Compatible Defenses Brace Yourself

By taking a moment or two, you can sight in with any ranged weapon, giving you a better chance to hit your target, however, there are diminishing returns after a bit.

Using this Tactic, you delay your next ranged attack by 3 seconds but gain a +5 to hit.

This Tactic can only be performed when the shooter is standing still. On top of this, the combatant takes 3 Stress when they finally make the shot.

Charge

Any Time Moving at >Jog

Compatible Attacks Feint, Flurry of Blows, Power Attack

A character using this Tactic must be moving at least a Run to throw their full force behind the below.

A charge is a terrifying tactic: if the blow lands, the defender takes 2d4! Stress and that damage is considered doubled for the purposes of determining Knock-Backs. Further, all allies of the defender take half the Stress of the defender.

The attacker then defends with a dice size 1 size smaller for 5 seconds or until they’re no longer Engaged.

Choking Up On Your Weapon

On Defense

Compatible Attacks All except Careful Aiming, Charge, and In Their Guard

Compatible Defenses All except Fend Off, and Long Guard

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

When you’ve choked up on your weapon, your weapon’s Measure is halved, and you add that number, rounded down, to its Recovery. Your halved Measure is considered your new Measure for determining who attacks first and what Tactics can be employed on you as well.

However, if an opponent is In Your Guard and you choke up on your weapon, you defend with a standard d20! and deal full weapon damage.

Feint

On Attack

Compatible Attacks Called Shot, Charge, In Their Guard

Compatible Defenses Choking Up, Hold Back, Long Guard

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

When performing this move, you (hopefully) get your enemy to commit his guard to a different location than you plan on hitting. You receive a +5 to that Attack but receive a -3 to Defense.

Fend Off

On Attack

Compatible Attacks Hold Back, Jab, Phalanx Fighting

Compatible Defenses Hit the Deck, Long Guard, Step Back

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

If your weapon's measure is at least 1 foot greater than your opponent's, you can try and fend them off. This means that you may attack them freely, while they can't do the same to you.

If you attack first when first closing with someone, you may forfeit that attack to automatically be Fending Off. After that, while Fending Off someone, you can attack as normal.

To become fully engaged with you, they'll have to knock your weapon aside with a regular attack that deals no damage. Should they hit, they become fully engaged and can attack as normal, although they need to wait their full weapon Recovery.

Until they do so, they’re only considered to be Threatening you so they’re unable to attack you directly.

You can fend off multiple people, however, they have to all be in front of you (up to 90 deg off center).

Flee

Any Time While Engaged

Compatible Attacks All except Charge

Compatible Defenses All

While engaged, you can turn around and move at a jogging speed or higher to attempt to disengage. This also applies to running by an opponent who Threatens you.

However, you open yourself up to an attack. If you have any foes who are within half of their weapon recovery they may make a free attack against you.

Note: because of the inherent risks involved in Fleeing, people will typically not choose to do so unless forced to by a mental break or some other means.

Flurry Of Blows

On Attack

Compatible Attacks Charge, In Their Guard

Compatible Defenses Brace Yourself, Choking Up

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

This tactic involves releasing a rain of quick attacks that rely more on speed than accuracy or power. This tactic can be declared any time after your attack if you're not using another Tactic that can't stack with this one.

While employing this tactic you attack at half speed, however, you suffer a -3 to Attack and Defense and your damage doesn’t explode.

Hit The Deck

Any Time

Compatible Attacks Called Shot, Hold Back, Jab, Phalanx Fighting

Compatible Defenses Active Dodging, Choking Up, Fend Off, Step Back

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

When a sudden arrow flies, there might be no other choice but to dive for it at all costs.

When you use this Tactic, you fall prone, gaining a +5 to your Defense vs ranged attacks.

However, if you dive behind non-shield cover, you also get the bonus for crouching behind it (although you're still prone).

This Tactic can be done when surprised.

Hold Back

On Attack

Compatible Attacks Feint, In Their Guard

Compatible Defenses All except Long Guard, and Step Back

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

Sometimes, you don't want to kill your enemy: you want to take them alive so you hold back.

When using this technique, dropping a person to 0 Vitality, doesn't kill them: instead it knocks them unconscious for 24 - their Con Stat hours.

However, when using this technique, halve the number of damage dice you roll (removing the smallest dice first), rounded up, and shrink the remaining by 1 size but this base damage is doubled for triggering Trauma.

So, using this strategy with a dusack results in 2d3! damage while using it with a Mace results in 1d10! Damage.

In Their Guard

On Attack

Compatible Attacks All except Careful Aiming, Charge, and Phalanx Fighting

Compatible Defenses Step Back

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

If you're engaged with a person and have a weapon that is at least 1 foot shorter than your opponent, you may attempt to get in their guard.

On a successful attack, the shorter weapon combatant has successfully knocked his foe's weapon aside and is now fighting at extreme close range, causing them huge penalties.

The attacker deals no damage but the longer weapon defends on a dice 1 size smaller against all attacks made against them and all damage dice is also reduced by one size (3d4! → 3d3!) on all attacks they make.

Jab

On Attack

Compatible Attacks In Their Guard, Phalanx Fighting

Compatible Defenses All

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

A strike that is meant to purposely to open your opponent’s guard and get them off their balance by making them shift their defense. This tactic can be declared any time after your attack if you're not using another Tactic that can't stack with this one.

The Jab attack happens at half weapon Recovery (rounded up) for Piercing and Slashing weapons. This can take them below their normal minimum Recovery, down to one.

Instead of a d20!, the defender uses a dice size 1 size smaller but on a successful hit, no damage is done, and the defender nets a -1 to their next defense against a non-jab attack.

This penalty doubles with each jab, however, it only lasts until the defender’s next full defense (so -2 for the second, -4 for the third, -8, etc.).

Crushing weapons may use this tactic but it’s not any faster than their regular Recovery.

Additionally, bonuses from all weapons don’t apply, unless they specifically reference the Jab Tactic.

Long Guard

On Attack

Compatible Attacks Feint, Jab, Phalanx Fighting

Compatible Defenses Active Dodging, Brace Yourself, Fend Off

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

By placing your weapon further out in front of you and trying to line the body of the weapon with the defender’s eye line, this may allow a person with a short weapon the first strike.

While using this tactic, you increase your weapon’s measure by 1/2 ft but you reduce your damage’s largest die by 1 size.

Crushing weapons are unable to employ this tactic.

Phalanx Fighting

Any Time

Compatible Attacks Power Attack, Jab, Long Guard

Compatible Defenses All except Active Dodging

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

This tactic requires you to have a piercing or slashing weapon that is equal to or greater than 5 + your partner's measure, but, if you have that, this allows you to attack over that partner in combat to reach enemies beyond you.

There is a trade-off. First you attack on 1 dice smaller. Second, for non-Crushing weapons, you reduce all damage dice by 1 size (removing any d3!s entirely). For Crushing weapons, you always only deal d4! of damage.

You can fight over multiple allies if your measure is larger than (5 * number of allies fighting over) + the front rank’s measure, however you shrink the Attack dice by 1 for every person you’re fighting over.

Power Attack

On Attack

Compatible Attacks Charge, In Their Guard, Phalanx Fighting

Compatible Defenses Brace Yourself

Other Compatible Flee, Shift

This is the fighting equivalent of baseball batting your enemy, swinging with all your might.

With this attack, you receive a -3 to your Attack and next Defense but your damage increases.

Piercing weapons increase 1 die by 1 size. Slashing weapons increase all dice by 1 size and crushing weapons increase its highest die by 2 sizes.

This only affects the base damage dice the weapon has: not damage added via Skills.

Shift

Any Time While Engaged

Compatible Attacks All except Careful Aiming, and Charge

Compatible Defenses All except Brace Yourself, and Step Back

While Engaged, you can move at your walking speed to either side of your opponent or change 1 facing per second but suffer a -3 to your next Attack and Defense. This penalty doesn’t stack.

However, if you Shift the same second you use another, compatible Tactic, you don’t receive this penalty and gain a +3 that can be either used to increase a bonus or decrease a penalty from the other Tactic.

Step Back

On Defense

Compatible Attacks In Their Guard, Phalanx Fighting

Compatible Defenses All except Brace Yourself

This is a defensive tactic that involves moving directly back from your opponent’s blows at your walking speed.

Before your defense roll, if you declare a Step Back, you receive a +5 to your next Defense roll and can decide to move directly back or back and to the side.

If you move directly back you move your Jogging speed backwards for 1 second.

If you move back and to the side, you move at a Walking speed for 1 second.

Either way, you gain a -3 to Attack. If you perform this Tactic multiple times between attacks, you gain an additional -3 to Attack for each time you do so.

If your opponent is using the Flurry of Blows, Feint, or the Power Attack tactic, if they do hit, you only receive half damage.
